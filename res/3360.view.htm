859<h2>

860FDA Strengthens Breast Implant Safety Requirements and Updates Study Results</h2>

861

862

863<p>

864On October 27, 2021, the FDA took <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="510e6591-2974-47b6-bc63-ef7e28db2069" href="/news-events/press-announcements/fda-strengthens-safety-requirements-and-updates-study-results-breast-implants" title="FDA Strengthens Safety Requirements and Updates Study Results for Breast Implants">

865several new actions</a>

866 to strengthen breast implant risk communication and help those who are considering breast implants make informed decisions.</p>

867

868

869<p>

870First, the FDA issued orders restricting the sale and distribution of breast implants to help ensure that patients considering breast implants are provided with adequate risk information so that they can make fully informed decisions.</p>

871

872

873<p>

874Second, the FDA approved new labeling for all legally marketed breast implants that includes:</p>

875

876

877<ul>

878<li>

879Boxed warning.</li>

880

881 <li>

882Patient decision checklist, which must be reviewed with the prospective patient by the health care provider to help ensure the patient understands the risks, benefits and other information about the breast implant device. The patient must be given the opportunity to initial and sign the patient decision checklist and it must be signed by the physician implanting the device.</li>

883

884 <li>

885Updated silicone gel-filled breast implant rupture screening recommendations.</li>

886

887 <li>

888Device description with a list of specific materials in the device.</li>

889

890 <li>

891Patient device card.</li>

892

893</ul>

894<p>

895The breast implant manufacturers have posted the updated device labeling to their websites.</p>

896

897

898<p>

899Lastly, the FDA released updated information on the status of breast implant manufacturer <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="39d87c91-707a-4ac7-a25f-4f339fd48d5b" href="/medical-devices/breast-implants/breast-implant-postmarket-safety-information#studies" title="Breast Implant Postmarket Safety Information">

900post-approval studies</a>

901.</p>

902

903

904</div>

905

906

907

908<p>

909<img alt="Hands holding a pair of breast implants." class="pull-right" src="/media/86714/download" title="Hands holding a pair of breast implants." />

910Breast implants are medical devices implanted under the breast tissue or chest muscle to increase breast size (augmentation) or to replace breast tissue that has been removed due to cancer or trauma or that has failed to develop properly due to a severe breast abnormality (reconstruction). They are also used in revision surgeries, which seek to correct or improve the result of an original surgery.</p>

911

912

913<p>

914There are two types of breast implants approved for sale in the United States: saline-filled and silicone gel-filled. Both types have a silicone outer shell. They vary in size, shell thickness, shell surface texture, and shape (contour).</p>

915

916

917<p>

918The information provided on this website is designed to support, not replace, the discussion that exists between a patient and a physician. We have provided this information to help women make informed decisions about whether or not to get breast implants:</p>

919

920

921<ul>

922<li>

923Explain the <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="e57ddc6b-12f6-46f2-a9cf-81a9b7c6eafa" href="/medical-devices/breast-implants/risks-and-complications-breast-implants" title="Risks and Complications of Breast Implants">

924risks of breast implants</a>

925 and describe the <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="2ae17f19-495b-406c-b052-5cd897c49a01" href="/medical-devices/breast-implants/breast-implant-surgery" title="Breast Implant Surgery">

926surgical procedures used</a>

927 to implant them.</li>

928

929 <li>

930Provide information on <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="91eae064-ba2b-44a8-b74e-024abc4fe718" href="/medical-devices/breast-implants/types-breast-implants" title="Types of Breast Implants">

931saline-filled and silicone gel-filled</a>

932 breast implants, including data supporting a reasonable assurance of safety and effectiveness, approval letters, <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="31dac5fa-9270-4819-8cc3-71fa1123e1bb" href="/medical-devices/breast-implants/labeling-approved-breast-implants" title="Labeling for Approved Breast Implants">

933labeling</a>

934 and information on <a href="https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cdrh/cfdocs/cfPMA/pma_pas.cfm?sb=pdn">

935post-approval studies</a>

936.</li>

937

938 <li>

939Provide information on <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="9003daf7-6d0b-49ff-b43f-6ea0b3233626" href="/about-fda/page-not-found">

940breast implant-associated anaplastic large cell lymphoma (BIA-ALCL)</a>

941 in women with breast implants.</li>

942

943 <li>

944Encourage patients to <a data-entity-substitution="canonical" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="0785221e-cef4-4465-8621-b77dcb1032b6" href="/safety/medwatch-fda-safety-information-and-adverse-event-reporting-program" title="MedWatch: The FDA Safety Information and Adverse Event Reporting Program">

945report adverse events</a>

946 associated with breast implants through the FDA's Medwatch</li>

947

948</ul>
